The CPU(Central Processing Unit)

The CPU is arguably the most important part of the computer, with it's importance tying only with the motherboard. The CPU is responsible for most calculations, its effectively the computer's brain. The better your CPU, the faster the computer can calculate whatever you desire, be it 1+1, or complex light and water physics, the CPU can do it. (This statement is not entirely true, different CPUS can vary in performance and cost, with some costing around $1000 in exchange for incredible performance). Compared to a human, it is the Parital Lobes of the computer.

The Motherboard

The Motherboard is the backbone that ties the computer's components together at one spot and allows the to communicate with each other. Without the motherboard, none of the computer parts (CPU, GPU, RAM, Hard drive) could interact. Without a working motherboard, a computer with eve the best parts is useless. It works by connecting all of the hardware to your processor, and it distributes the electricity to all of the hardware. Compared to a human, it is the Nervous System of the computer.

The GPU(Graphics Processing Unit)

The Graphics Card/ Video card is a specialized processor designed to accelerate graphics rendering. They are able to process many pieces of data at the same time, making them incredibly useful for machine learning, video editing, and most kinds of offline/online gaming. Some graphics cards with the RTX pretext have special hardware and software that allows it to simulate rays, with light rays being what most people use it for. Compared to a human, it is the Optical Nerve of the computer.

The SSD (Hard Disk Drive)/ SSD (Solid State Drive)

The SSD and HDD are what stores and retrieves digital data in a computer. The main differences between the two are that SSDs are faster, non-mechanical, shock resistant, last-longer, are better for gaming, are more effeicient, and are more reliable. HDDs are cheaper, and are better suited for stroing extra data, like movies, photos and documents. All in all, SSDs are better for performance, but it doesn't really matter if you just use your computer casually. Compared to a human, they are the Hippocampus of the computer.

The RAM(Random Access Memory)

RAM is the hardware in a computer where the operating system, application programs and data in current use are kept so they can be quickly reached by the device's processor. There are different types of RAM, those being SRAM(Static Random Access Memory), DRAM(Dynamic Access Memory), and ECC(Error-Correcting Code). SRAM and DRAM are two classifacations of memory, with ECC just being a type of DRAM that has an additional cell to detect and correct random faults. With SRAM, data is stored using a six transistor memory cell. DRAM store data using a transister and capacitor pair, which makes up one DRAM cell, is is cheaper that SRAM, but it is also slightly slower than SRAM. Compared to a human, they are the Pre-Frontal Lobe of the computer.

The PSU(Power Supply Unit)

The PSU converts alternating high voltage current(AC) into direct curr(DC), they also regulate the DC output voltage to be what the computer can handle. You could also use a UPS(Uninterruptible Power Supply), it is essentually a power supply that saves some electricity for when the power fails, it will then start supplying the computer with said backup electricity for a few minutes, the transition is near instantaneous. The electricity is stored within batteries, supercapacitors, or flywheels. Compared to a human, it is the Heart of the computer.
